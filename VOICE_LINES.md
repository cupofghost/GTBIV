> excluded** — Kimi's handling his lines exclusively. Deb is included
> below: SpeechGen is now generating her audio too. Turbo's line packs
> still live in `CHAPTER1.md` §8 (new material) and the shipped
> `TURBO_LINES` array in `index.html` (existing material), if needed later.

> character's sheet, then every line they speak, cleaned of stage

## Donna Marinara
**Voice direction:** calm, composed, never raises her volume — all menace
delivered flat, entirely in food metaphor. Think quiet and clipped, not
sultry or cartoonish.

1. "You've been sampling my delivery fleet, Turbo."
2. "That's a violation of the family recipe. Next slice you steal, I take something back. Something you'll miss more than a pizza."
3. "You have odd taste in delivery vehicles, Turbo."
4. "Return my scooter. I will not ask a third time."
5. "Every crust has its limit."
6. "This isn't a rivalry. It's quality control."
7. "I hope you like the taste of consequences."
8. "A man who steals pizza cars has never truly tasted pizza."
9. "I built this fleet from a single scooter and a grudge. Don't test me."
10. "You are not a customer. You are a supply-chain issue."
11. "Even my dough has more backbone than your excuses."
12. "I've buried men for less than a stolen breadstick."
13. "I warned you once. Warnings are a courtesy. I'm out of courtesy."
14. "You're not on my menu anymore, Turbo. You're the special."
15. "Somebody bring me a box. A big one."
16. "Last time was a conversation. This time is a delivery — of a different kind."

## Detective Frank Hardcastle
**Voice direction:** gravelly hardboiled-noir monologue, said completely
straight and out loud, even in broad daylight. He means every word.

1. "The city was a jukebox of sin, and Turbo Jones was its greatest hit."
2. "Another night in San Chaos. Another Jones."
3. "I've seen a hundred guys like him. I've caught none of them."
4. "Dispatch, this is Hardcastle. Tell my captain I said... nothing. Tell him nothing."
5. "Somewhere, a donut is getting cold. It'll have to wait."
6. "The city doesn't forgive. Neither do I. Probably."
7. "I used to believe in the system. Now I believe in overtime pay."
8. "They call him Turbo. I call him Tuesday's problem."
9. "My captain says let it go. My captain's never lost a jukebox to a guy in a stolen sedan."
10. "Every siren in this city sings the same sad song. I just hum along."
11. "This is it. This is the one. It's always the one, until it isn't."
12. "Dispatch, requesting backup. And maybe a therapist."
13. "If I don't get him tonight, I retire. I say that every night."

2. "You body-slammed my quarterback over a Gatorade, Jones. You're not a victim. You're a liability with a grudge."
3. "Rules haven't changed. You want back on my field, you go through me. Fists only. Let's go."
4. "...huh. Still got it, Jones. Still stupid. But you got it."
5. "Fine. You're off The List. Field's open. Don't make me regret this."
6. "Absolutely not."
7. "PICK UP YOUR FEET, JONES!"
8. "That's the same sloppy footwork that cost you the season!"
9. "I've benched better hits than that!"
10. "You call that a punch? I've been hit harder by a cold front!"
11. "TWENTY YEARS and you STILL lead with your chin!"
12. "...alright. Alright! Down. You're down— I mean, I'm down."
13. "Get up. No — stay down a second. Then get up."
14. "Fine! FINE! You've got heart, Jones! Misplaced, but heart!"
15. "Head up, JONES! Eyes downfield!"
16. "That's it! That's what I've been trying to teach you for TWENTY YEARS!"
17. "Don't you dare fumble in front of me again!"
18. "ATTA BOY— don't let it go to your head."

## Group voices

These roles are archetypes, not named individuals — one or two voice
takes per group covers all their lines. Listed by group.

**Voice direction:** loud, dumb-muscle energy — the comic contrast to
Donna's cool composure.

1. "Boss said no discounts!"
2. "You're not on the delivery schedule, pal!"
3. "This one's extra crispy!"
4. "Nobody steals the family car!"
5. "That's a FAMILY vehicle, chump!"
6. "You want a tip? Here's my fist!"
7. "Boss is gonna hear about this — and so are your kneecaps!"
8. "Get off our route, freeloader!"

**Voice direction:** cocky ex-jock, half taunting and half genuinely still
sore about Danny — louder and dumber than the pizza goons, slapstick
rather than menacing. Warms up noticeably in the last group of lines
(post-Rematch/Turbo Bowl).

1. "Hey, it's Fumble Jones!"
2. "Coach still talks about what you did to Danny's knee, man."
3. "You still owe this team an apology. And a knee replacement."
4. "Nice bus pass, star player."
5. "Heard you're back. Heard nobody cares."
6. "You get banned from the locker room AND the whole city? Impressive."
7. "This is for Danny's knee!"
8. "You're not even wearing pads, this is EASY!"
9. "Coach said if I see you, I hit you. Simple as that."
10. "Should've stayed inside, Jones!"
11. "...respect, man. Actual respect."
12. "Coach never lost a fistfight before. You broke something sacred."
13. "You're still banned from the group chat, though."
14. "Not today, Jones!"
15. "You're still slow, old man!"
16. "I've got you, I've got you— dang it, I don't got you!"
17. "Coach is WATCHING, Jones, don't embarrass us!"

### Boardwalk Racer
**Voice direction:** cocky but harmless — a bragging-rights rival, not a
real threat.

1. "Bet you can't catch air off that ramp without eating pavement."
2. "My grandma drifts better than that."
3. "Sixty seconds. Make it count, hotshot."
4. "Pink slips are so last decade. Just embarrass yourself for free."
5. "I've seen shopping carts drift better."
6. "Land it clean or don't land it at all."

### Cheer Squad (ambient, non-Amber)
**Voice direction:** light and gossipy, mostly indifferent to the general
chaos of San Chaos.

1. "Two, four, six, eight, who do we NOT appreciate? Coach's whistle, that's who."
2. "Is that guy allowed back here?"
3. "He's kind of got main-character energy, not gonna lie."
